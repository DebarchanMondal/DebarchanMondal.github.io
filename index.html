About Me

I am a research scholar working at the intersection of space microbiology, robotics, bioinformatics, and scientific instrumentation. My research focuses on understanding microbial adaptation and behavior under microgravity conditions, with particular emphasis on structural, genetic, and functional changes. I have hands-on experience in the design, maintenance, calibration, and troubleshooting of scientific instruments, as well as in developing robotics-based experimental platforms.

My bioinformatics expertise includes genome analysis, molecular dynamics simulations, and systems biology approaches to explore microbial mechanisms at a systems level. By integrating wet-lab experimentation with computational modeling and custom tool development, I strive to advance space biosciences and support translational research.

I am especially passionate about developing innovative, low-cost scientific tools and technologies that enhance experimental capabilities, particularly for researchers facing funding constraints. My primary objective is to provide practical solutions, either through experimental research or by designing custom scientific instruments.
